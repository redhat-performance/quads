The purpose of the Scale Lab and the QUADS scheduler is to automate all aspects of machine and network provisioning to provide Engineers with an efficient high-performance testing grounds for scaling our products for short-term workloads.

There are just a few guidelines to using resources within the environment:

  • Please Do Not Rename Servers

    • We have a very specific naming convention that helps us know where a server is located, what rack, U-location and type. This helps us quickly address any physical hardware issues or problems that might arise. While renaming your machine doesn’t affect DNS records it may cause confusion when reporting issues.
    • Please also do not rename servers in Foreman, this will affect DNS/DHCP records and cause issues for us and other tenants in the lab.

 

  • Do not Share Environments for other Purposes

    • Please do not share credentials with other people for purposes other than the stated cloud workload assigned to you. This is bad for both tenants waiting for new hardware as well as our metrics which feed into Engineering purchasing decisions. Each Scale Lab workload is for a targeted, singular purpose and not meant to change hands after your work is completed. If someone wants your systems after you’re done (even on your team) let us know and we can see about changing ownership and updating the runtime records for a seamless transition assuming we do not have a large backlog of requests that have been waiting.

  • Use a New Assignment / Request for New Versions, Components or Change in Focus

    • Please open a new request if the major version changes, components change or overall focus of your testing changes. We can easily move your current systems to a new assignment with no downtime and also keep all data intact. Most of the time this takes 5minutes or less but it’s important to keep our product reporting accurate and helps you too for making sure your work is as visible as possible.

 

  • Do not Change or Edit Foreman Entries or Hardware

    • Access to cloud Foreman views is meant for self-service re-provisioning of systems. Beyond marking systems for build or changing their intended operating system documented in the FAQ do not rename, delete or otherwise edit physical or network attributes about hosts in the Scale Lab. This can be disruptive to not only your assignment but also others.

  • Do Not Upgrade Hardware Firmware or BIOS

    • Firmware and BIOS updates can have permanent, lasting effects on tenants after you, their test results (which are often shared with customers) and makes it hard for us to keep baseline consistency and behavior on bare-metal systems. If you think you need a newer version of BIOS, Firmware, NIC firmware or any sort of micro-code update please let us know.
    • We have a process for doing this to ensure changes are tested, documented, and applied consistently across all similar model types. There are lots of reasons we may need to revisit newer versions of this kinds of changes but Scale Lab tenants should not under any circumstance take this upon themself to apply to temporary, loaned systems in the R&D shared labs.
    • If you think you might require a newer NIC, System or component firmware or BIOS for your work please open a request in our Perf-Infra JIRA and tell us the system(s) and current cloud and we can work together to get this tested and completed.

  • Please Do Not IP VMs or Systems Manually

    • We have a tightly designed network infrastructure shared and used by potentially hundreds of ongoing workloads. With the exception of optional routable public VLANS where you have a specific range of IP addresses allotted to you, do not manually IP any VM’s created or systems loaned to you – an IP address conflict can take down the entire R&D infrastructure and cause disruption to your colleagues.If you require additional temporary DHCP records for VMs in your environment please contact us.

  • Please Do Not Change IPMI Passwords

    • Scale Lab resources change hands often, but don’t worry you’ll get your own unique credentials for the duration of your request. Please do not change or modify any IPMI/iDRAC passwords.

  • Make sure to Unmount Virtual Media ISO if used.

    • While we have a fully automated provisioning system you can use via Foreman explained in our FAQ, there might be times when you want to load a virtual CDROM ISO image. Please remember to unmount this after you are using it so it does not cause problems with the next tenant to use the systems after you.

  • Do not Change IP Address of Public Network or IPMI/Out-of-Band

    • Please do not change the IP address settings of either the Public Network of any of your systems or the IPMI/out-of-band interfaces. This can severely disrupt all running assignments and infrastructure in the Scale Lab in the case of IP address conflicts. These are managed via DHCP and should not be touched or modified and there’s no practical reason for doing this.

  • Do Not Change Host Foreman Root Passwords

    • Just like IPMI passwords, please leave the per-host Foreman root passwords alone, you can change them as you like once you get your systems but modifying this value will cause problems for the next tenant after you.
    • Your host password is 100yard- until you change it otherwise at the OS level once your cloud has been validated and handed off for you to use.

  • Do Not Change Interface Ordering or PXE Settings

    • Our automation (and the successful handover of machines to the Engineers after you) relies on automation tooling to set boot order, interface order and other aspects of bare-metal systems a certain way.Under no circumstances should you disable PXE or re-order boot interfaces on the systems, this causes delays and issues with sharing the Scale Lab equipment with others after you are done. We have self-service tooling/automation in place for toggling boot order for OpenStack vs. Everything else scenarios – see the FAQ for more information about this.

  • Do not make changes to System BIOS

    • There is really no reason anyone needs to make BIOS level changes, if changes are made at the BIOS level this can cause issues for you but also other Engineers that use these systems after you. It can hold up our validation processes and automation that gets machines/networks to people that need them. If you think you need to make BIOS adjustments please let us know first. We will work with you to ensure you have what you need.A very small, small minority of tenants may need to make adjustments to test very myopic, focused areas of CPU architecture, if you’re in this situation please mention it on your Scale Lab assignment request form and we’ll ensure you have what you need to be successful. While we do not block access to BIOS please try to consult us first if you think something needs to be changed. At the least please revert any changes made before your assignment ends and your work is complete.
    • If you’re looking for a way to blacklist or disable some hardware (like a PCI RAID controller + disks) you can employ rdblacklist=megaraid_sas in your kernel command line boot in your image or OS for example.

  • Please Do Not Run DHCP/PXE On Primary Public Interfaces

    • For the lab to run we manage our own Foreman instance that provides DHCP/PXE/TFTP services on the primary (public facing 10.1.x) interface on all of the machines. Running any sort of DHCP/PXE or Multicast traffic here can be disruptive to the entire lab. You will have anywhere from 2-4 internal, high-speed isolated interfaces in their own VLAN for segregated layer2 traffic to do whatever you like. As a courtesy we also lay down 172.16.x interface configs for you to use in this manner across the internal interfaces.
    • Please consult our FAQ or Tips and Tricks documentation for further information.
    • Authoritative Interfaces Diagram Here

 

 

  • We will Pro-actively Shut Down Potentially Abusive Traffic

If you have any questions at all about network traffic, or what you can or cannot do please ask us. Unfortunately, if we detect any network loops or traffic flooding into the lab we will shut down your assignment and disable the ports until we can rectify the problem. Usually this is due to improperly serving things off of the Public Network.

 

There is just too much disruption that can happen otherwise and we almost always have dozens or more of other sensitive workloads within the R&D environment that cannot risk disruption due to network or service mis-configuration.

 

We’d rather be safe than sorry, and in the past we’ve seen absolute havoc wreaked by tenants inadvertently running broadcast-level traffic or other kinds of activity in their assignments which can cause routing loops and other impactful consequences.

  • OpenStack Users: Always use instackenv.json provided by QUADS

    • We auto-generate and maintain an instackenv.json in a column to the right of your assignment summary. Please use this to feed into RHOS Director for OpenStack deployments.

  • Machine Allocations are a Shared Resource

    • Scale Lab is the AirBNB of high-performance testing so be a good neighbor.

  • Resources are Limited

    • We will always face greater demand than supply, please be patient when requesting access.

  • Bare-Metal as a Service Only

    • While we’re happy to provide advice we do not setup or manage services, application infrastructure or offer support beyond bare-metal systems and network problems. Once you receive your machines they are yours to configure, setup and run your workloads. The high level of automation and rapid provisioning, hand-off and reclamation of assets doesn’t lend to supporting anything beyond the operating system or self-service provisioning. There are efforts underway to automatically deploy some common application stacks like OpenStack automatically via QUADS when you get your systems but this is not in place yet.

  • We Cannot add Capacity or Hardware to Systems

    • Hardware capabilities, to include extra disks, storage or anything else is what comes on your system. We do not have the supportable capability to expand hardware beyond what comes on systems. From time to time we will refresh hardware, order additional disks to expand slots and the like but it’s not something tenable we can do during the lifetime of tenant assignments.

  • Scale Council Drives Priority

    • The Scale Council is comprised of Engineering managers who will ultimately decide the priority of machine allocations, which are subject to change. Your friendly DevOps team (Will and Kambiz) just make things work.

  • Vetted Workloads Only

    • Scale Lab is designed for already-proven workloads that need to be tested at a higher scale. This isn’t the right environment for small-scale or untested R&D sandbox development as allocations are short-term. For example you might discover bugs in a product on a small set of Eng-Ops provided hardware and need to prove this on faster networks and larger machine counts.
    • We currently have a minimum request limit of 10 machines, if you need less than this number then you should checkout the BAGL Lab or ALIAS Lab instead.

  • No Long-term Reservations

    • Scale Lab is not intended for long-term, multi-month reservations. An ideal place for this kind of workload is the Eng Ops/PNT supported DSAL labs where the default run time is 4 months.
    • We will always try to make accommodations and if both resources and priority of your request allow we’ll make things work but run times longer than 2month is the exception not the rule.

  • Singular Purpose or Focus for Workloads

 

    • Scale Lab is intended for singular-purpose workloads only, if your focus changes or pivots we need a new request. You can likely use the same systems you were using however, pending availability.
    • This includes testing a new major version of products or pivoting to new testing regimens and scope.
    • More information here on this perf-dept mailing list thread.

 

  • Support is available Monday-Friday

    • Just like you we try not to work over the weekend, if you have issues within the Scale Lab please let us know and we’ll get to you first thing Monday morning European time.

  • Please Respond to your Request Emails ðŸ™‚

    • There’s a lot of demand for the Scale Lab and we’d love to service everyone but it’s just not possible. Things move fast so If we don’t hear from you via JIRA requests in 30 days your request will be closed.
